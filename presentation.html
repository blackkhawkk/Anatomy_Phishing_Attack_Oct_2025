What is Phishing? üé£

üéØ Critical Terminology

Phishing is a subset of Social Engineering - the psychological manipulation of people to perform actions or divulge confidential information.

Professional Definition

Phishing is a social engineering attack vector where cybercriminals impersonate trusted entities through digital communications to steal sensitive information such as credentials, financial data, or personally identifiable information (PII).

1

Deception

Attackers create fraudulent communications mimicking legitimate sources

2

Trust Exploitation

They impersonate trusted entities: banks, employers, service providers

3

Information Harvesting

Victims are manipulated into divulging sensitive data or credentials

üö© Red Flags Identified

  • Flattery bombardment - Excessive praise to lower guard
  • Urgency pressure - "24 hours", "time-sensitive"
  • Vague company details - No specific company name
  • Suspicious domain - ".net" instead of company domain
  • Shortened URLs - Hides real destination
  • Too good to be true - $200K+ salary offer

üéØ Psychological Manipulation

  • Professional ego appeal - "impressive background"
  • Career advancement desire - High salary promise
  • FOMO tactics - Limited time, exclusive opportunity
  • Authority figure - Fortune 500 company claim
  • Confidentiality allure - Makes victim feel special
  • Social proof - LinkedIn connection status

üõ°Ô∏è Protection Tips

  • Verify the sender - Check their actual LinkedIn profile
  • Research the company - Look up official domains
  • Never click suspicious links - Type URLs manually
  • Trust your instincts - If it seems too good to be true...
  • Contact through official channels - Use company websites
  • Be skeptical of urgency - Legitimate offers don't rush
