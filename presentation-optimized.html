üé£ What is Phishing?

Definition

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, and personal data.

üé≠ Deception

  • Fake emails, websites, or messages
  • Appears to come from trusted sources
  • Uses familiar logos and branding

üéØ Target

  • Personal information
  • Login credentials
  • Financial details
  • Corporate data

üí∞ Goal

  • Identity theft
  • Financial fraud
  • Corporate espionage
  • Data breaches

üìä Phishing by the Numbers

üåê Global Impact

  • 3.4 billion phishing emails sent daily
  • 90% of data breaches start with phishing
  • $12 billion lost annually to phishing

üìà Growing Threat

  • 65% increase in phishing attacks (2023)
  • 1 in 4,200 emails are phishing attempts
  • 32% of successful attacks lead to data breaches

üéØ Success Rate

  • 30% of phishing emails are opened
  • 12% of users click malicious links
  • 3% provide their credentials

üé≠ Types of Phishing Attacks

üìß Email Phishing

  • Mass emails to many recipients
  • Generic, untargeted approach
  • Most common type of attack

üéØ Spear Phishing

  • Targeted at specific individuals
  • Personalized and researched
  • Higher success rate

üêã Whaling

  • Targets high-profile executives
  • High-value financial impact
  • Sophisticated social engineering

üì± Smishing (SMS)

  • Text message based attacks
  • Mobile device targeting
  • Often uses urgency tactics

üìû Vishing (Voice)

  • Phone call based attacks
  • Voice impersonation
  • Creates false sense of urgency

üåê Clone Phishing

  • Copies legitimate emails
  • Replaces links with malicious ones
  • Appears authentic and familiar

üîç 1. Reconnaissance

üé≠ 2. Impersonation Setup

  • Website cloning and hosting

üìß 3. Initial Contact

üé£ 4. Hook & Payload

üí∞ 5. Data Harvesting

  • Financial data extraction

üîÑ 6. Exploitation

üö© Critical Red Flags to Watch For

üìß Email Red Flags

  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Urgent language ("Act now!", "Limited time")
  • Threats ("Account will be closed")
  • Grammar/spelling errors

üîó Link Red Flags

  • Mismatched URLs (hover to reveal)
  • Shortened links (bit.ly, tinyurl)
  • Suspicious domains (typos, extra characters)
  • HTTP instead of HTTPS
  • Unexpected redirects

üìé Attachment Red Flags

  • Unexpected attachments
  • Suspicious file types (.exe, .zip, .scr)
  • Double extensions (file.pdf.exe)
  • Password-protected files
  • Executable macros in documents
