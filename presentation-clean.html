üé£ What is Phishing?

üìã Definition

Phishing is a cybercrime technique where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, and personal data.

üé≠ Key Characteristics

  • Deception: Fake emails, websites, or messages
  • Urgency: Creates false sense of immediate action needed
  • Impersonation: Mimics trusted brands and organizations
  • Social Engineering: Exploits human psychology

üìä Impact Statistics

  • 83% of organizations experienced phishing attacks in 2024
  • $12 billion in losses annually worldwide
  • 1 in 4 employees click on phishing links
  • 30% of phishing emails are opened by targets

üéØ Types of Phishing Attacks

üìß Email Phishing

  • Mass emails to thousands of recipients
  • Generic messages with malicious links
  • Fake login pages and attachments
  • Most common type of phishing

üé£ Spear Phishing

  • Targeted attacks on specific individuals
  • Personalized content using research
  • Higher success rate than mass phishing
  • Often targets high-value employees

üêã Whaling

  • Targets high-profile executives
  • Sophisticated social engineering
  • Business email compromise (BEC)
  • Highest financial impact

üì± Smishing (SMS)

  • Phishing via text messages
  • Mobile-specific vulnerabilities
  • Shorter attention span exploitation
  • Growing threat vector
